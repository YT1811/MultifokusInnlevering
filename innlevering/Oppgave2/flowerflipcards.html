Rose

Did you know that roses are over 35 million years old? They’ve been around for a long time, with fossil evidence showing they existed in ancient times!

Tulip

Tulips were once so valuable in the 17th century that they caused a financial craze known as "Tulip Mania," where a single bulb could sell for more than the price of a house!

Hibiscus

Hibiscus flowers are not only beautiful but also useful! In many cultures, the petals are used to make herbal teas known for their refreshing taste and potential health benefits, like lowering blood pressure.

Sunflower

Sunflowers can grow incredibly fast—some varieties can grow up to 12 inches in a single day! They’re also known for their "heliotropic" behavior, meaning they turn to face the sun throughout the day.

Lavender

Lavender isn’t just pretty—it has a soothing scent that’s been used for centuries to reduce stress and anxiety, and even promote better sleep!
